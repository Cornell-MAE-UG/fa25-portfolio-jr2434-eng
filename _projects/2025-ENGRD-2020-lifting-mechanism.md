A challenge provided at the end of Homework 5 for ENGRD 2020 - Statics and Mechanics of Materials was:
Given a 2D design space of 150cm long and 50cm tall, a rigid bar of a fixed length (your
choice), 3 pin supports of which two need to be mounted on the ground and a linear
actuator picked from an online catalog, design a
frame/mechanism to lift the maximum possible weight to the highest possible height.
Assume all the supports and bar/actuator are rigid.

This is how I solved the problem:

The website provided was a set of actuators from Tolomatic. I picked the actuator with the highest force and largest stroke length to provide the greatest possible lift for the block on the bar. This was the high force rod-style actuator with a force up to 294 kN and a stroke length of 1.5 m.

Using the two pins that we are allowed to use on the floor, I connected one to a side of the member, and the other to other for the linear actuator. The weight is attached to the other side of the member which is attached via a taught rope to the linear actuator. Since the weight cannot balance at the edge of the member, I created a 45 degree angle on the actuator and member. This would achieve the highest weight lifted to the highest height.
